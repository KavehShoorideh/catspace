# Forcing Regions, Not States: Adversarial Committor Geometry for Planning in Two-Player Games

*catspace technical report — 2026-07-16. A merged and empirically grounded synthesis of
two development threads: a formal derivation of adversarial reachability geometry, and a
measured experimental program in chess. The derivation supplies the theory (what breaks when
single-agent reachability meets an opponent, and the repairs that restore it); the experiments
supply the evidence — and, in three places, force corrections the theory alone did not see. Formal
statements follow the derivation draft; every quantitative claim is a printed script verdict from
the experimental thread, cited inline. Figures are generated by
`experiments/viz/merged_paper_figures.py` from those verdicts.*

---

## Abstract

Chess engines are superhuman yet cannot state a plan, because they do not have one: an engine
emits a move, and its principal variation is a prediction of play, not a decomposition of the task.
In trivially won positions the win probability saturates, search loses its gradient, and the engine
shuffles — precisely where a club player converts with a plan stated in intermediate *situations*:
confine the king, bring the pieces over, shrink the box, mate. We develop planning on a learned
**adversarial reachability geometry**: an asymmetric distance whose length is the negative log
probability of *forcing* one situation from another. In this geometry a plan is a path, a threat is
the opponent's short path, defense is lengthening it, and — we prove — subgoals must be *regions*
of positions, not single positions, because the opponent chooses the reply. Two poles (our mate,
their mate) and a third draw region emerge from the data with no bespoke loss; the model's
calibrated uncertainty about its own distances decides when to calculate and when to trust
judgment; and search over exact dynamics grounds the field into an episodic memory that
generalizes the transposition table by *reachability* rather than identity.

The contribution of this document over the pure derivation is empirical, and it is not only
confirmation. Measurement establishes that the reachability distance does **not** automatically
avoid the shuffling regime: in a guaranteed-win region the near-goal field is flat (Spearman
−0.01 against true distance-to-mate), and the gradient that *should* order it exists in the data
(Spearman +0.29) but is **generated by the draw walls** and erased by any history-blind
aggregation, representation, or search. This relocates a central design claim from "automatic" to
"conditional on augmented state," and names the fix. Measurement also localizes a capacity
pathology (the learned metric occupies ~7 of 64 dimensions regardless of width, and continued
training overwrites rare regimes as undefended collateral) and disciplines the design with two
falsified components (a categorical distributional head whose entropy *anti-correlated* with
sharpness; post-hoc distillation that improved every field metric while never improving play). We
give the merged architecture, the theory that constrains it, the experiments that corrected it, and
a pre-registered validation program.

## 1. Introduction

A search-based engine, asked what to do, answers with a move: the root action with the most
visits or the best backed-up evaluation. Its principal variation follows best moves for both sides
down the tree — a *prediction* of play, not a decomposition of the task. It contains no subgoals,
no statement of *why* the position is winning, and no object a teacher could point at. The gap
shows exactly where engines are strongest: in a trivially won endgame the win probability
saturates near 1 for every reasonable move, the search loses its gradient, and the engine shuffles.
A human converts the same position with a plan stated in intermediate *situations* rather than
moves, and the plan is robust to the defender's choices because each stage is a family of positions
one can steer into whatever the opponent does.

This report develops a framework in which such plans are the primary object. It merges two
threads. The first is a formal derivation: it identifies what breaks when single-agent reachability
methods meet an opponent, constructs the repairs, and proves the internal-consistency results
(soundness of plan budgets, non-worsening of search, validity of memory bounds) that state what
would have to hold for the method to work. The second is an experimental program in chess that
built and measured these components — and it is reported here in a specific epistemic register: it
confirms parts of the derivation, **falsifies** two of its candidate components, and forces one deep
correction to the derivation's central promise. We keep the two registers explicit throughout, so
that a reader can see which claims are theorems (internal consistency), which are measurements
(with confidence intervals and anytime-valid evidence values), and which remain hypotheses.

The framework relates to quasimetric goal-conditioned RL [Wang–Torralba–Isola–Zhang 2023;
Liu et al. 2023; Wang–Isola 2022] — the line establishing that the optimal goal-conditioned
value function *is* a quasimetric — but carries it into a discrete, adversarial, DAG-structured
domain where those methods have no prior validation, and it adds what the adversary and the
domain force: an opponent-conditioned two-pole field, region-valued subgoals, and a distance
defined as forcing *improbability* rather than shortest-path length.

## 2. Reachability as geometry

The primitive is an asymmetric distance `d(s → g)`: how hard it is to force `g` from `s`. Asymmetry
is the substance, not a technicality — chess is saturated with irreversibility. A queen given up in
one move is not un-given by any; the distance from a middlegame to a simplified won ending may
be five plies while the distance back is infinite. A distance that is zero from a position to itself
and obeys the route-stitching law `d(x → z) ≤ d(x → y) + d(y → z)` is a **quasimetric**, and the
stitching law is the algebra of plans: going via `y` is one way of getting from `x` to `z`, so the
direct difficulty can never exceed the sum.

Two versions of "how hard" matter, and the framework keeps them distinct (a discipline whose
violation is a category error — Assumption A10). The **theoretical** version counts plies against
best defense,

```
d*(s → G) = min_{our strategy} max_{their strategy} (plies until the game enters G),
```

which for `G` = "positions where we deliver mate" is the tablebase's distance-to-mate (DTM). The
**practical** version asks how likely we are to get there against *this* opponent, in *this* state —
rated 1500, rattled, low on clock — bundled into a conditioning variable `θ` (in the experimental
code, `ω`) covering both players:

```
d_θ(s → G) = − log P_θ(s ⇝ G).                                              (1)
```

The logarithm turns probability into geometry: chances along a plan multiply while distances
add, and `−log` converts one into the other. Equation (1) is the same object statistical mechanics
calls a **committor** — the probability of hitting one absorbing set before another under the
process's own dynamics — and framing it that way is what makes "reach mate" and "reach a draw"
a single uniform mechanism (§4).

The practical field has **two poles** — the set where we mate (`T_P`) and the set where we are
mated (`T_O`) — and one geometry holds both distances for every position. Three structural facts
follow (Figure 1), each corresponding to something a player already knows.

![Two-pole geometry](figures/mp_two_pole.png)

*Figure 1. The two-pole committor geometry. Poles are mutually unreachable (distance ∞ both
ways) by absorption alone — no training term separates them. Basins may overlap: the contested
ridge contains sharp positions close to both mates, which is signal (double-edged), not a defect to
regularize away. Draws form a third region far from both poles: a fortress is a place from which
neither mate is reachable, a different fact from being in the defender's winning basin. A loss that
sorts positions by eventual winner would fabricate a partition the game does not have and misfile
every dead draw (Proposition C.4; experimental corroboration: an outcome-region probe found win
and loss states intermixed even four plies from forced mate, silhouette ≈ 0).*

## 3. Subgoals are regions, not positions — a theorem

This is the observation on which the design turns, and it is where the derivation is most
advanced: it is not a convenience but a mathematical necessity. In a one-player world you may set
any state as a goal and go there. In a game, the opponent owns every other move, so to force play
into a single prescribed position, *every* defensive branch would have to reconverge to exactly
that diagram — which generically fails. You can force the enemy king to the edge; you cannot
force it to h8, because the king chooses which way to run. What is forceable is the family.

**Observation 3.1 (point-to-set is the native type; Appendix Observation B.3).** For a singleton
`{g}`, `d*(s → {g}) < ∞` demands that every defensive branch reconverge to `g`. Whenever a
forcing path passes an opponent-to-move state with two successors that do not both admit forcing
lines back to the same position, `d*(s → {g}) = ∞` — even from completely winning positions.
Adversarial reachability is therefore natively a function `S × 2^S → ℕ ∪ {∞}`, and

```
d*(s → G) ≤ min_{g ∈ G} d*(s → {g}),   typically with strict — often infinite — gap.   (2)
```

![Region necessity](figures/mp_region_necessity.png)

*Figure 2. The opponent's branching makes singleton subgoals generically unforceable. After the
forcing move `a`, the defender chooses among replies landing in different members of the region `G`,
none selectable in advance. Play is steerable into `G`; it is not steerable to any single `g`.*

Every stage of textbook technique — cut off the king, win the pawn, reach the Lucena structure —
names a family and never a diagram, and (2) is why. It dictates the engineering: goals passed to
the model, anchors in memory, and planner nodes are all *sets*, and the budget for continuing a
plan from a region is charged from the worst position the defense might have steered us into.
Under that honest accounting the stitching law survives (Appendix Lemma B.5), and its survival
is what later makes plan budgets **certified** rather than hopeful (§6).

**Experimental note (a scar that confirms the theorem the hard way).** An early implementation
represented the mate goal as a single averaged vector, then as a *bank* of exemplar mate positions
scored by soft-min. Both failed at play: the centroid's distance was flat against true mate-distance,
and the exemplar bank *lost decisively* (three pre-registered rejections, e up to 65), because
"closest exemplar" changes move-to-move and injects goal-switching noise into move ranking. The
lesson matches the theorem's engineering corollary: a region must present a smooth, descendable
surface (a committor field over the set), not a point and not a bag of points.

## 4. Architecture

One encoder digests a position into a latent; everything else reads geometry off it (Figure 3).

![Component diagram](figures/mp_component.png)

*Figure 3. One encoder feeds a quasimetric head and per-surface committor heads; the entropy /
neighbor-disagreement signals modulate search; search over exact dynamics grounds values into an
episodic memory, distilled slowly back into the encoder.*

**The distance head.** The distance is an **Interval Quasimetric Embedding** (IQE)
[Wang–Isola 2022]: a valid quasimetric *by construction* — the triangle inequality is architectural,
so the model can never assert `x` is far from `z` while a route through `y` is short — and universal
(any quasimetric on a finite set is representable). *Which thread is ahead here:* the derivation's
IQE strictly dominates the metric-plus-bilinear-residual (MRN-style) score
`f·W·g − ‖σ⊙f − σ⊙g‖` the experimental thread actually ran; IQE is asymmetric by construction,
where MRN bolts asymmetry on via `W`. The merged design adopts IQE and retains, from the
experimental thread, the *per-dimension metric scales* `σ` — because those scales are the object
the capacity fix prices (§7).

**Committor heads.** Terminal outcomes are absorbing surfaces; for each we learn the hitting
probability directly:

```
d_W(s) = −ln P(hit mate-for-us first),  d_D(s) = −ln P(hit a draw first),  d_L(s) = −ln P(mated first).   (3)
```

There is **no goal vector**: the surface enters only as a boundary condition supplied by the rules
engine (a rollout terminated; the rules say which surface it crossed, *anywhere* on it — touchdown
semantics). This dissolves a whole bug class (no goal vector to mismatch or average) and makes
the draw field `d_D` first-class: the "out-of-bounds" surface a losing side steers *toward* and a
winning side keeps clearance *from* (load-bearing in §6). The win surface is a *union* — mate,
opponent flag-fall, opponent resignation — recorded as distinct sub-classes (they are reached by
different plans) while `d_W` targets `P(any win)`; human resignations carry exogenous noise ("wanted
a London, got an Italian") and are routed to an "other" class rather than taken as ground truth.

**The distributional head — and a falsification the derivation must absorb.** The derivation
proposes a single categorical head `Z(s, G)` over ply counts plus an "unreachable" atom, whose
three read-outs recover the field (unreachable mass), rank subgoals (mean), and signal sharpness
(entropy). It is an elegant unification. *But the experimental thread built it and measured its
entropy anti-correlating with true sharpness* (ρ ≈ −0.2 across readouts, versus a weak +0.14 for a
plain point-head spread), and killed it at a short run before wasting a long one. The resolution is
already latent in the derivation's own Remark G.2 ("what the spread means depends on the labels"):
under human-play/ply-gap supervision the entropy is *aleatoric* opponent variability, not the
*epistemic* sharpness the search wants; the two are different objects a single categorical head
cannot separate. **Merged position:** keep the categorical head only where its supervision is a
Dirac target under exact truth (small-board tablebase distances), where entropy is genuinely
epistemic; for the practical field, obtain sharpness from a *multi-ε identification* (below) and
uncertainty from *neighbor disagreement* (§5), which indicts the metric itself. This is a case where
the experimental thread is ahead: it has a measured negative that redirects the design.

**Sharpness, measured.** Estimating `−ln P̂` at several execution-noise levels `ε` and regressing
`−ln P̂(ε) ≈ E(s) + S(s)·ε` per state identifies a path-existence term `E` and a **sharpness** slope
`S`. Measured over 4,373 states, `S` is essentially uncorrelated with distance-to-mate (Spearman
+0.04): risk does not accumulate with length, it concentrates in bottlenecks — the annotator's
intuition ("a long quiet technique is safe; a short sharp one is not") quantified, and a direct
refutation of any constant length/risk trade.

## 5. Training, and the two-ply adversarial stitch

Training adapts the constrained recipe of quasimetric RL: *stretch all distances apart as far as
possible, subject to short stitches along real transitions.* Local structure is enforced by the
stitches; global separation is the objective and expands exactly where no chain of stitches holds —
which is how the poles, basins, and draw region of §2 emerge with no bespoke loss terms.

**Why one ply is the wrong stitch (derivation, Lemma F.1).** The single-agent stitch — one
move, cost one — silently assumes we *arrive* at the position after our move. In a game we never
do: the opponent moves next. Counting an attack as an achievement without asking what comes
back is the beginner's error, and one-ply stitches sew it into the geometry. The smallest unit
about which anything can be guaranteed is a **move and its reply**, so stitches span two plies:

```
d_φ(s, s'') ≤ 2,   s'' = position after our move a and the reply the current model deems worst,   (4)
```

imposed per candidate move `a`. Two honest costs (Appendix Remark F.2): the worst reply is
judged by the model being trained, so training is a monitored fixed-point iteration, not a certified
procedure; and the trained distance is meaningful *state-to-region*, the only query the system
makes, not between arbitrary single-position pairs.

*Which thread is ahead here:* the derivation. The experimental thread trained with in-batch
InfoNCE plus a ply-gap regression — a one-ply, retrieval-style objective — and ran head-first into
exactly the pathologies the two-ply stitch is designed to prevent (a flat near-goal field, §6). The
merged training rule is the two-ply adversarial stitch; the experimental thread's contribution here
is the *evidence* that skipping it produces the predicted flatness.

**Committor-in-the-base-objective (the recipe that actually moved play).** Alongside the stitched
quasimetric, the outcome committor is trained as a first-class term in the base objective
(`L = L_stitch + λ_p · CE(head(F(s)), result)`), not distilled afterward. This is the strongest empirical
result of the program: a full-board-trained outcome head, read as a committor, converts a
tablebase-won toy endgame **0.78 vs 0.64** for the point-goal readout at deep search (n = 200,
CI [+0.070, +0.215], anytime-valid e = 91), with faster mates. It is *not* a value head reintroduced:
the learned objects are navigable geometries with plan semantics, and the scalar the planner acts
on (§6) is composed at readout from the game's known scoring rule, not trained.

**A falsified alternative (banked).** The experimental thread also ran a 12-round closed loop that
generated own-play data and *distilled* it into the field post-hoc. It compounded every field metric
and never improved play (root-conversion 0.719 → 0.719 → 0.672 across pre-loop / round-1 /
round-12), because re-fitting a fixed embedding on an accumulating table warps the regions play
traverses. **Merged position:** train the committor *in* the base objective; do not iterate post-hoc
distillation. This is a second measured negative that disciplines the design.

## 6. The rim is flat — and why (the deep correction)

The derivation's §7 promises that, unlike an engine whose win probability saturates in won
positions, "a reachability distance keeps decreasing monotonically to mate, so every hop
measurably makes progress precisely where engines lose their gradient." **This is the one place the
experimental thread contradicts the derivation, with tablebase ground truth, and the correction is
deep enough to change the architecture.**

Measured on a pawnless rook endgame (KRR-vs-k, where the tablebase gives exact
distance-to-mate), the learned committor is **flat** near the goal: Spearman(d_W, DTM) = −0.01
(CI [−0.11, +0.09]) over the mate-in-1 to mate-in-8 band, and at the decisive positions the field
*prefers* tempo-wasting moves (the best progress move ranks #7 of 18). Yet the gradient the field
*should* have learned exists in the data: empirical conversion `P̂` falls 0.87 → 0.73 across the same
band (Spearman +0.29, CI [+0.16, +0.51]).

![Wall-generated gradient](figures/mp_wall_gradient.png)

*Figure 4. Left: the committor target has a real gradient — nearer-mate positions convert more
often. Right: the learned field is flat against the same truth. The gap is not a training failure to fit
a signal; it is that the signal is destroyed before the field can see it.*

The resolution is the paper's sharpest new idea. In a *guaranteed-win* region every move wins
under perfect play, so per-position conversion is near-constant — the gradient cannot come from
"which move wins." It comes from the **draw walls**: under fallible play, wasting tempo risks the
50-move and threefold surfaces, and that risk is the *only* thing making a slow move worse than a
fast one when the win is otherwise secure. The gradient is therefore a first-passage property of the
**augmented** (history-bearing) state, and it is erased in three places, each now a named fix:

1. **Aggregation.** `P̂` keyed by board identity averages a position reached fresh with the same
   position reached mid-shuffle, blurring the repetition history the threefold wall lives in.
   → key targets by `(position, repetition-count)`.
2. **Representation.** A repetition-count input plane is added (state = `board × counters`, so the
   threefold surface is representable), but heads trained on history-blind targets leave it inert.
   → train on repetition-keyed targets.
3. **Search.** In-tree positions historically carried no move history, so the search cannot detect a
   threefold forming *inside its own lines*. → path-aware terminal detection in search.

This is precisely the derivation's Assumption **A1** ("repetition history excluded"), which the
derivation flags as a simplification and the experiment shows to be **load-bearing exactly where the
framework claimed its advantage**. The correction also elevates `d_D` from a convenience to a
*sensor*: a clearance term `reach = −d_W + β·d_D` uses distance-from-the-draw-basin to break ties
where `d_W` saturates, funneling play down the mate corridor. The winning side erects the
threefold wall *behind* itself; the losing side navigates *toward* it. The monotone-descent promise
is true — but only for the augmented-state committor with visible walls, not for the raw field.

## 7. Capacity: wide representation, priced metric

A second measured pathology, invisible to the derivation, explains a recurring "play peaks early
then degrades" phenomenon (a lightly-trained checkpoint mates a rook endgame its own
long-trained continuation shuffles into a draw). The learned metric occupies an effective **~7 of 64
dimensions regardless of width** (effective rank 5.7 → 6.9 across a full run), and in the final
training stretch — whose gradients contain *zero* rook-endgame information — rook-endgame
features drift **1.7×** more than middlegame features.

![Capacity forensics](figures/mp_capacity.png)

*Figure 5. Left: the metric never uses more than ~7 of 64 dimensions, from the first snapshot to
the last. Right: late training drags the rare (endgame) regime 1.7× more than the common one. The
rare regime is undefended collateral — nothing in the frequent data anchors it, so shared-parameter
updates overwrite it.*

The architectural response decouples *representational* capacity from *geometric* capacity: a much
wider embedding with an **L1 tax on the per-dimension metric scales** `σ`, warmed in after an
exploration phase — *the representation is free; the metric is priced.* Distance dimensions are
allocated per-pattern, so specializing on the frequent regime no longer requires overwriting the
rare one; regimes can occupy disjoint sparse supports. A complementary defense is a small replay
anchor of endgame data so rare features have gradients *defending* them, not merely capacity
insulating them. Whether ~7 dimensions is the objective's demand or a contrastive-collapse
pathology is the open question a wide+sparse run (in progress) is built to answer; the pre-registered
gates are that effective rank rises and scales with width, the drift ratio flattens toward 1, and
rare-regime competence survives to late steps. *This is entirely the experimental thread's
contribution; the IQE-based derivation does not address it, and the merged architecture needs it
whichever distance head it uses.*

## 8. Grounding: search, memory, self-improvement

A learned field is locally wrong somewhere, and games offer a corrective learned-model domains
never enjoy: the exact transition function is free. Search expands the true game tree and consults
the field only at leaves, which changes its epistemic status entirely.

**Proposition 8.1 (non-expansive amplification; Appendix H.2).** With alternating minimax
backup of leaf estimates each within `ε` of truth, the backed-up root value is within `ε` of truth —
min and max are non-expansive in sup norm, and per-ply constants preserve differences. *Planning
over a learned model can seek out and amplify the model's own optimistic errors; backing values
up an exact tree provably cannot.* Search improves the field exactly when the leaves are better
known than the root — which the stopping rule enforces if confidence is calibrated.

**Search-to-certainty** stops each line only at certainty: a grounded leaf (mate/tablebase found
in-tree, exact) or a learned-confident leaf (low entropy, trusted). Sharp lines run deep; quiet lines
stop early (Figure 6) — quiescence generalized, and the human discipline of "calculate until you
reach a position you can assess with a clear conscience."

![Search-to-certainty](figures/mp_search_to_certainty.png)

*Figure 6. Variable-depth search: sharp lines extend to ground truth, quiet lines stop at a
confident head. Provenance records which kind of leaf each backup rests on; distillation weights
grounded provenance above merely-confident, so regions whose searches never touch truth cannot
drift on self-generated authority. Empirical corroboration: replacing beam-minimax with PUCT
MCTS over the same field is a confirmed, e-value-gated play improvement — the search that reads
the geometry matters as much as the geometry.*

**Episodic memory generalizes the transposition table.** A transposition table returns a value on
exact re-encounter; the memory answers the more useful question — *have I solved a position this
one can force its way into?* — retrieving by reachability. The two directions of the asymmetric
distance **sandwich** the unknown value between known ones (Appendix Lemma I.2): if I can force a
transposition into a mate-in-`m` within `k` plies, my mate is at most `k + m` away; and a solved
position that forces its way into mine within `k'` plies cannot be more than `k'` closer to mate than I
am. The interval width is an honesty meter — act on tight sandwiches, search on wide ones. Better,
the geometry polices itself (Appendix Lemma I.3): near-neighbors must carry near-equal values, so
wild neighbor disagreement is a *proof* the map glued together positions it should have kept apart
(a stalemate trap filed among routine wins) — a sound, localized detector of embedding error, and
the epistemic-uncertainty signal that survives the categorical head's falsification (§4).

The loop closes at two speeds: memory is fast (a value grounded this session serves neighbors
immediately), the field is slow (memory distilled into weights, grounded provenance weighted
above confident). Under stated conditions (Appendix H.4) the trusted region grows *backward from
checkmate* — basic mates, then elementary endings, then technique positions — the order humans
learn the game.

## 9. Planning: regions, threats, repertoires

**Concept regions** are sets cheap to shuffle among and expensive to leave — a purely metric
signature (cheap inside, expensive across the boundary), so regions are discoverable by clustering
the learned geometry with no one hand-coding "confinement"; human-readable names are attached
afterward by probing simple features, but no planning step consumes the names. A **plan** is a path
through regions, each edge budgeted from its worst entry point.

**Proposition 9.1 (plan soundness; Appendix J.3).** A plan's total budget is a certified upper
bound on the true distance-to-mate (up to estimation error); any plan of finite certified cost is a
proof of a win. The box method becomes a theorem — confine, approach, shrink, mate, guaranteed
whatever the defender does — which is exactly the property that has always made technique
teachable. Execution is by short searches region-to-region; on defender deviation the high level
replans from the position on the board — the plan survives even though the move sequence does
not ("I play by plans, not variations"). *This is where the merged design's payoff meets §6's
correction: a certified region plan is only descendable if the field is non-flat along it, which is
exactly what the augmented-state committor with draw-wall clearance restores.*

**Everything dualizes.** Run the planner on the opponent's pole and the output is *their* best plan
— the threat, an inspectable chain of regions — giving defense a geometric definition (lengthen
their shortest plan: prophylaxis) and principled pruning (a wing-pawn push that moves neither field,
while we stand mated in three, never enters the search). Because the head predicts distributions,
plan selection is risk-sensitive: a plan's ply count is the convolution of its hops, and choosing by
the mean versus the bad tail (conditional value-at-risk) is the pawn-up decision — optimize the tail
and squeeze when ahead, head for the sharpest mess when worse. One knob carries the style axis.

**Conditional repertoires.** Because chess alternates, a fixed line cannot refute a fixed line: "for
every plan of theirs, a refutation" is not a sequence but a **conditional repertoire** — a small
covering set of plans plus a recognition rule. Formally a small game in plan space: populations of
our plans and theirs, a payoff per pairing, a Nash mixture computed and stress-tested by a
double-oracle loop (each side's planner best-responds to the other's mixture) with a classical
convergence guarantee, terminating when exploitability falls below tolerance. The opponent's moves
are descent steps in *their* geometry, so a short prefix reveals which plan they execute (Bayesian
recognition) and the prepared counter deploys before the threat lands. The layer is aggressively
cacheable — region-to-region budgets are position-independent, the recognition posterior updates by
one Bayes step per move, and only plans whose regions a move touches need re-examination.
*Which thread is ahead here:* the derivation, entirely — the experimental thread has no plan-space
meta-game yet; it is the richest untested layer, and the merged program's most ambitious frontier.

## 10. What this buys, and what is proven

Against **AlphaZero-family engines**, the deltas are the plan itself (a chain of inspectable regions
with a budget, plus the dual chain that is the threat) and — *conditional on §6* — a gradient that
descends to mate where win-probability saturates. Against **planning over learned world models**,
the delta is epistemic: their search can amplify model errors; ours provably cannot worsen leaf
accuracy (Prop. 8.1), available in any domain with a checkable simulator. Against **single-agent
quasimetric RL**, the delta is the opponent and it is structural: one-ply constraints become
unsound (§5), singleton subgoals unreachable (§3), a second pole appears (§2) — those repairs are
the portable content. Against **tablebases**, the delta is reach: the memory's sandwich bounds
interpolate outward from exact boundaries with certified two-sided error.

**Confirmed (e-value-gated, deterministic-defender paired playouts, pre-registered confirmatories):**
certainty-in-base-objective beats the pre-certainty field at full board (composed e = 539); the
committor readout beats the point-goal readout (e = 91 at n = 200); MCTS beats beam-minimax at
matched compute; sharpness ⊥ distance (ρ +0.04); the wall-generated near-mate gradient (ρ +0.29)
versus the flat learned field (ρ −0.01). **Falsified and banked:** the categorical head as a sharpness
detector; post-hoc distillation as a play-compounding loop; goal-as-region *readout* (centroid and
bank). **Motivated, in progress:** the wide+priced-metric capacity fix; the three wall-visibility
fixes converting the flat rim; the plan-space meta-game.

**What we do not claim:** reliable small-budget conversion of even a simple won endgame. That is
the standing gate, and every element above serves clearing it.

## 12. Validation program

Factored into independently-falsifiable hypotheses, cheapest first; on the small board everything
has computable ground truth (retrograde DTM, exact outcome probabilities), so the first tier is
direct comparison to exact solutions, not benchmark scores. Comparative claims run under
anytime-valid sequential statistics; selected effects require a pre-registered confirmatory on a fresh,
single-use position set; a source-inspecting leakage audit gates every run (engines and tablebases
grade and oppose, never train).

- **H1 Geometry** — the field recovers true reachability (agreement with DTM and exact outcome
  probabilities; dead draws far from both poles; two-sided positions finite to both). *Status: the
  rim sub-case is measured flat (§6) — the falsifier fired, and named its cause.*
- **H2 Calibration** — head entropy is a valid regime signal after recalibration; the confidently-wrong
  rate gates the loop. *Status: end-to-end likelihood training collapsed rank; monotone
  (isotonic) post-calibration works (ECE 0.23 → 0.06, rank-exact).*
- **H3 Regions** — clustering discovers confinement / approach / shrink without supervision,
  beating random-encoder and raw-board nulls.
- **H4 Self-grounding** — the search-and-memory loop improves the field with tablebases only as a
  withheld referee; sandwich intervals cover at their claimed rate.
- **H5 Planning** — region plans with replanning convert at least as reliably as flat search at
  matched compute, without the shuffling regime, producing inspectable plans. *Status: the
  prerequisite (a non-flat rim) is the current build; the planner comparison follows.*
- **H6 Practical field** — the `θ`-conditioned field predicts real outcomes and the risk knob behaves
  as designed, beating material and engine-eval baselines.
- **H7 Structure** — the specific commitments earn their place (IQE-vs-MRN; two-ply-vs-one-ply;
  wide-priced-vs-narrow metric; committor-in-base-vs-distilled), each ablated at matched capacity.

Failure of any one is informative and non-fatal to the others. What would falsify the framework as
a whole is the conjunction failing where its conditions demonstrably hold — calibrated head,
accurate local metric, augmented state with visible walls — since under those conditions the
theory shows the pieces must compose.

## 13. Contribution

1. **Adversarial committor geometry.** A two-pole, opponent-conditioned reachability field whose
   distance is `−ln P(force the goal)`, a quasimetric by the multiplicativity of path probabilities,
   with draws as a third region — repairing the min-semantics optimism a shortest-path or
   contrastive distance bakes in. *(Derivation formal; experiment confirms full-board, composed
   e = 539.)*
2. **Subgoals are regions — necessarily.** The point-to-set gap (2) as a theorem, with the smooth
   committor-surface corollary the goal-bank failure confirmed empirically. *(Derivation.)*
3. **The wall-generated near-mate gradient.** The measured correction that reachability does *not*
   automatically descend to mate: in a won region the gradient is a first-passage property of the
   *augmented* state, generated by the draw walls and erased by history-blind aggregation,
   representation, and search — turning a notorious "flat near the goal" failure into a precise,
   fixable signal-flow problem. *(Experiment; corrects the derivation's central promise.)*
4. **Free representation, priced metric.** Decoupling representational from geometric capacity (wide
   embedding, L1-taxed metric scales) so rare regimes need not be overwritten to serve frequent
   ones — motivated by a measured ~7-dim collapse and 1.7× rare-regime drift. *(Experiment.)*
5. **Two-ply adversarial stitches, IQE, search-to-certainty proofs, sandwich memory, and the
   plan-space meta-game** as the formal architecture; and the **experimental harness** —
   deterministic-defender paired playouts, anytime-valid e-values, pre-registered single-use
   confirmatories, source-inspecting leakage audit, and capacity forensics — as the methodology
   that lets an overnight run produce a week of trustworthy verdicts. *(Both threads.)*

The honest status: a principled, measured, largely-built architecture for planning-by-navigation
whose individual mechanisms are each validated or each falsified-and-corrected, whose central
known failure now has a mechanism and a fix under test, and whose richest layer (the plan-space
repertoire) is derived and awaiting its first experiment.
